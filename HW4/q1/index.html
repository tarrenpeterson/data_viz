Obamacare achieved its stated goal

At the start of his term, Barack Obama inherited an insurance landscape in which nearly 40% of all low-income individuals were without insurance. His signature domestic policy achievement was the passage of the Affordable Care Act (ACA), popularly referred to as Obamacare, a law that aimed to increase health insurance coverage for low-income and previously uninsured people.

The chart below shows the type of insurance that Americans had across the income spectrum. It first shows this information in 2009, when Barack Obama was inaugurated, and updates to reflect how the same information has changed in 2020, ten years after the passage of the ACA.

As shown in the graph above, Obamacare made significant progress in reducing the number of uninsured Americans throughout the income distribution. However, this improvement was most pronounced amongst those Americans with the lowest incomes, with only 15% remaining uninsured. This achievemnt was largely driven by the expansion of Medicare and Medicaid and access to insurance exchanges.
